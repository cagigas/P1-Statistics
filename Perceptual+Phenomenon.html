5. Now, perform the statistical test and report your results. What is your confidence level and your critical statistic value? Do you reject the null hypothesis or fail to reject it? Come to a conclusion in terms of the experiment task. Did the results match up with your expectations?¶

\begin{equation*} H_o = Congruent\ and\ incongruent\ reaction\ times\ do\ not\ differ.\ \mu_c=\mu_i\\ H_A = Congruent\ and\ incongruent\ reaction\ times\ do\ differ.\ \mu_c<\mu_i\\ t = \frac{\bar{x}-\mu}{SE}\\ SE = \frac{SD} {\sqrt{n}} \end{equation*}

We'll suppose a confidence level of 0.05. We will conduct a one-tailed test, as the incongruent test will have larger reaction time as congruent.

Using the t-table, the t-critical value at an alpha level of 0.05 with df=23 t=1.714

If we substract one normal distribution from another, the new mean will be \begin{equation} \bar{x}_D = \bar{x}_1 + \bar{x}_2 \end{equation}
